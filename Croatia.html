Croatia

I studied abroad in Dubrovnik, Croatia during spring break in 2024. I received credit for the Global Marketing course. After my experience in Dubrovnik, I learned the value of collectivism and indirect communication.

Things I Noticed about Croatia

Collectivism

In Croatia, people value collectivism. Croatians are very loyal and family-oriented. They show long-term commitment to members of their family and extended family. While being in a collectivist culture, I learned to be more considerate of others whether it is learning a few words in the Croatian language or speaking quietly on buses.

Individualism

In the United States, people value individualism. Individuals are most likely to prioritize themselves over a group. In the United States, people focus on personal achievement and happiness.

Indirect Communication

Unlike the U.S. culture, Croatians often favor indirect communication. For instance, disagreements may be softened and requests may be phrased politely instead of directly stated. Nonverbal cues such as body language, facial expressions, and tone of voice carry significant weight in communication. After my experience in Croatia, I learned to be mindful of nonverbal cues and avoid being overly assertive to adapt to their indirect communication culture.
