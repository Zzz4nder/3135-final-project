France

I studied abroad in Paris, France during spring break in 2024. I received credit for the Global Marketing course. After my experience in Paris, I learned to value polychronic time and direct communication. Although the U.S. culture is mostly direct, it was a great experience to see what it is like from a different perspective.

Things I Noticed about Paris

Polychronic Time

"The belief that time is an unlimited good and available as needed"

In France, the culture is easygoing and time is not very strict. For example, being 15 minutes late is normal and not considered impolite. It is also normal to sit at lunch for 2 hours with friends because, in the French culture, they value experience and focus on the present moment.

Monochronic Time

"The belief that time is a precious good and should not be wasted"

In the United States, time is valuable and it is important to not waste it. In the U.S. culture, most things are planned and it is critical to always arrive on time. For example, arriving to a meeting on time is important or it can come off as impolite or irresponsible.

Direct Communication

In France, people are direct and honest. They are not afraid to share their opinions and will give you an honest answer to questions. Due to the direct style of communication, it has often been misunderstood as impolite. The perception of the French coming across as rude is a stereotype that has developed over time. This stereotype may come from cultural differences in communication styles. For example, directness and assertiveness in communication, which are common in the French culture, may come across as impolite to individuals from other cultures. It is important to accept and understand cultural differences to avoid cross-cultural barriers.
