My Second Home

UNC Charlotte has not only offered me classes that can be challenging, but has also provided me with the opportunity to create lifelong friends whether through study abroad , activities on campus, or in a classroom. Below are some of the co-curricular activities I have been able to participate in and grow as an interculturist throughout my journey in the GESP.

International Festival

The International Festival is a yearly event hosted by UNC Charlotte. This festival celebrates diversity, music, and food from over 50 countries. There are also educational activities, dance performances, and much more during the event. After attending this event, I was able to learn more about different cultures such as traditional clothing, cultural performances, and unfamiliar food. I was able to try a Korean dish that I had never tried before and I really enjoyed it! This event is very important to the campus and locals because it provides the opportunity to participate in different cultural traditions and exposure to diversity.

Friendship and Cultural Exchange Program (FACE)

This semester I joined the FACE Program and I highly regret not participating sooner. This program provides the opportunity for UNC Charlotte and international students to meet weekly and participate in activities to learn more about one another and build friendships. Through this, I made friends from Palestine and India and was able to learn more about each other.

International Coffee Hour

International Coffee Hour is an event that takes place on the first and third Thursdays of the month. This event provides the opportunity for UNC Charlotte and international students to meet, play games, and have great conversations. Through this, I was able to build cross-cultural friendships and gain more knowledge about different cultures from countries such as the Philippines and India.

Descriptions!

The International Festival is held on the UNC Charlotte campus in and around the Barnhardt Student Activity Center (SAC). The International Festival is centered around booths arranged in colorful marketplace style representing the cultures of over 35 nations. The booths are staffed by UNC Charlotte international students and members of Charlotteâ€™s international community and feature art, crafts and costumes from each participating country. Many booths offer international food for sale. Festival entry and parking are FREE! The International Festival is open to the public. We highly recommend you consider bringing cash with you to the event. While some booths may accept credit cards, many only accept cash; there are several ATMs on campus, but in past years, the lines can be quite long!
Throughout the day the music and dance of a variety of nations will be presented on indoor and outdoor stages. In addition, the International Festival, which is family-oriented, offers a number of elements such as mimes and face painting designed especially for children.
